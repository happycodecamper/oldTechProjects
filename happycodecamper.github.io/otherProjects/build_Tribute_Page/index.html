Louis Pasteur

The Father of Microbiology

“ In the fields of observation chance favors only the prepared mind. ”

“ Let me tell you the secret that has led me to my goal. My strength lies solely in my tenacity. ” Louis Pasteur

His Life

  • 1822 - Born on December 27th in the town of Dole, France to a family of a poor tanner.
  • 1827 - Family moved to Arbois, France.
  • 1829 - Student at École Primaire, Arbois. His teachers considered him an average student with a passion for drawing and painting with a keen eye for detail and precision. His teachers encouraged him to follow his artistic skills, but his father considered painting an indulgence.
  • 1840 - Student at Collège Royal de Besançon, France: Bachelor of Arts degree.
  • 1842 - Student at Collège Royal de Besançon, France: he earned Bachelor of Science degree.
  • 1847 - Student at École Normale Supérieure (Paris): he earned his Doctorate degree.
  • 1848 - Began his career as a professor of chemistry at the University of Strasbourg, France and became Chair of Chemistry in the faculty of sciences
  • 1849 - Married Marie Laurent, the daughter of the university's rector, and had five children, though only two survived childhood. While attempting to solve a problem concerning the nature of tartaric acid found in the wine fermentation, his observations led to a new field called stereochemistry.
  • 1854 - Appointed dean of the science faculty at the University of Lille where he worked on research to find solutions for the manufacturers of alcoholic drinks and found out that organisms such as bacteria were responsible for souring wine, beer and even milk.
  • 1857 - He moved to Paris where he became the director of scientific studies at the École Normale Supérieure, working from 1858 to 1867, and introduced several procedures to improve the standard of scientific work. During that time, he developed ideas that the alcoholic fermentation was due to yeast of beer, found everywhere that sugar is decomposed.
  • 1859 - Pasteur lost his daughter to typhoid – a disease caused by dirty food and water.
  • 1862 - Completed on April 20th, the first test of a process to remove bacteria from milk by heating it to a temperature of 60 -100 Celsius Degrees and then cooling it. The process is known as pasteurization. He disproved the theory of spontaneous generation: “There is no known circumstance in which it can be confirmed that microscopic beings came into the world without germs, without parents similar to themselves”.
  • 1863 - French vineyards were losing money when wine bottles spoiled in transit. Pasteur realized that this was due to contamination, but boiling wine to kill bacteria made it taste terrible. In a series of careful experiments, Pasteur discovered that heating wine to 55 Celsius Degrees killed bacteria without ruining the its taste.
“ At this point, Pasteur’s career does seem to have been guided by the special providence that watches over a genius ” Patrice Debré
  • 1865 - He rescued the French silk industry by proving that an infection in the silkworms was transmitted by parasites, and showed how infected silkworms could be isolated and destroyed. At this time, Pasteur lost his second daughter who died due to typhoid as well.
  • 1866 - Pasteur lost his third daughter who died from a tumor.
“ The only thing that can bring me joy is work ” Louis Pasteur
  • 1868 - Pasteur suffered a stroke, which partially paralyzed his left side. Colleagues set up a mobile laboratory so he could work from his sickbed.
  • 1870 - He discovered the cure for anthrax – a disease that mainly affects cattle. He found that by giving cattle a weakened form of the illness they were able to develop immunity to the illness -, and the cure for rabies – a very common disease at the time using similar principles he developed a weakened strain of the disease.
  • 1888 - Created the Pasteur Institute in Paris whose purpose was to continue research into infectious diseases, such as diphtheria - a major childhood killer. Today around 85% of children around the world are immunized.
  • 1895 - Died at the age of 73 due to the deterioration of his health and a second stroke.
His principles were used by later scientists such as Frankland, Valley Radot, Emile Duclaux, Descours and Holmes in developing vaccines for diseases such as typhus, diphtheria, cholera, yellow fever and different strains of plague.
